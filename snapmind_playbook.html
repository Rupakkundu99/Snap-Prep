Q&A prep — click each question to see the ideal answer
"Why not just use Notion AI or Perplexity?" ▶
Those are general-purpose. SnapMind is context-aware of your specific research session. It knows the 30 pages you've read this week, cross-references them, and builds a knowledge graph. Perplexity forgets you after every query. Notion AI only knows what's already in Notion.
"How do you handle hallucination?" ▶
Every answer is grounded in indexed content with block-level citations. The cross-encoder reranker removes low-relevance chunks before they reach the LLM. Context deduplication removes contradictory near-duplicate passages. And the model is explicitly instructed to say "I don't know" if context is insufficient.
"What's your embedding model and why that one?" ▶
Gemini embedding-001 producing 3072-dimension vectors. Chosen for multilingual capability and because it outperforms OpenAI's ada-002 on MTEB benchmarks for document retrieval. We have a Mistral fallback if Gemini is unavailable.
"How does the multi-agent system avoid getting stuck?" ▶
Hard context budget of 8000 characters. Once it's full, synthesis triggers regardless. The SlicerAgent also caps each scraped page at 6000 characters — so one bad URL can't blow the whole budget. Plus exponential backoff on every API call with a 5-minute hard timeout on crawl jobs.
"Is it production-ready?" ▶
Production-grade architecture: ThreadPoolExecutor for parallel embedding, exponential backoff on all DB operations, HNSW indexes for sub-millisecond vector lookup, connection pooling via psycopg, full Docker deployment, and 12 test files covering every pipeline component. It's running on Hugging Face Spaces right now.
"What's next on the roadmap?" ▶
Collaborative research notebooks for teams, autonomous agents that can fill in forms and navigate dynamically, and fine-tuned domain-specific embedding models. The SnapMind AI CLI is already on npm — same RAG pipeline, terminal-native with LanceDB for fully offline use.
